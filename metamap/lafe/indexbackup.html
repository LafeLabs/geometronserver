Circumference of the Earth:

40,000 km $\equiv$ 360 degrees(lattitude)

This was the original definition of the meter: one 40 millionth of the distance from the equator to the poles. This has long since been replaced by a definition involving the speed of light and the atomic clock, but it stood for some time, and the original definition required epic adventures across a large fraction of the globe to measure the relationship between distance and angle.

Lattitude is the distance in angle units described here from the equator, and generally has either a N or S to denote if it's North or South of the equator. In computer software north and south are generally just denoted by sign: plus above equator south below.

Longitude is the angle around the world from that of Greenwitch, England. Unlike the equator, this is an arbitrary political decision, based on the historical naval power of the British Empire. Other prime meridians have been used, but again for the purpose of computer software it's fixed in England. Anything between 0 and 180 degrees west is marked as "W", but again in comptuer software it's just denoted by sign. So everything in the Americas is negative, most of Europe is positive, as is all of Asia.

1 arc minute of lattitude $\equiv$ 1 nautical mile

1 degree $\equiv$ 60 arc minutes

$$1 \textrm{ mile}\left(\frac{1 \textrm{ NM}}{1.1508\textrm{ mile}}\right)\left(\frac{1\textrm{ deg}}{60\textrm{ NM}}\right) = 0.014482\textrm{ deg}$$ $$\approx 0.015\textrm{ degree lattitude}$$ $$\textrm{1 foot} = \frac{\textrm{0.015 deg}}{\textrm{5280 feet}} \approx 0.000003^{\circ}$$ $$\textrm{1 yard} \approx 0.00001^{\circ}$$ $$\textrm{10 yard} \approx 0.0001^{\circ}$$ $$\textrm{100 yard} \approx 0.001^{\circ}$$ $$\textrm{1000 yard} \approx 0.01^{\circ}$$ Longitude degrees are multiplied by cosine of the lattitude. Washington, DC is at approximately 39 degrees north lattitude, and the cosine of that is about 0.777 or about 78%(down by 22%). 60 degrees North or South is 50% down, which is roughly Anchorage, Alaska or Oslo Norway(give or take a couple degrees).
